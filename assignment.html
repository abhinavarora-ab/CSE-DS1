GLOBAL WARMING

What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term rise in the Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels, which release carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

effects of global warming.

  • Burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas)
  • Deforestation (cutting down trees that absorb CO2)
  • Industrial processes that emit greenhouse gases
  • Waste management practices that release methane

Effects of Global Warming

Global warming has a wide range of negative impacts, including:

  • Hotter temperatures. As greenhouse gas concentrations rise, so does the global surface temperature.
  • Increased frequency of extreme weather events (storms, heatwaves, floods)
  • Melting of ice caps.
  • Loss of species.
  • Disruption of ecosystems and agriculture

What Can We Do to Stop Global Warming?

There are several actions we can take to reduce the impacts of global warming, including:

  • Switching to renewable energy sources (wind, solar, etc.)
  • Reducing waste and recycling
  • Planting trees to absorb CO2
  • Using energy-efficient appliances
  • Reducing our carbon footprint by using public transport or electric vehicles
